# Cookie Choices

_Chocolate chip? Oatmeal? Advertising?_

**Goal**

To observe how cookie consent mechanisms affect which cookies are stored in your browser, and how different consent choices change the outcome.

**Instructions**

1. Open your web browser in **Incognito or Private Browsing mode**. This helps ensure a cleaner state.
2. Navigate to [https://www.dw.com/](https://www.dw.com/)
3. **Before** interacting with the cookie consent banner (it usually appears at the bottom or as a pop-up):
    - Open your browser's developer tools (usually `F12` or right-click -> "Inspect").
    - Go to the "Application" tab (Chrome) or "Storage" tab (Firefox).
    - Under "Cookies," select the domain for [https://www.dw.com/](https://www.dw.com/).
    - Take a screenshot and note down any cookies already present (names, and if possible, their apparent purpose based on the name, e.g., "__cmpcccx").
4. Now, interact with the cookie consent banner.
    - **First Interaction:** Look for an option like "Customize cookies," "Cookie preferences," or similar (the exact wording may vary).
        - If available, try to _reject all non-essential_ cookies or only accept "necessary" or "functional" ones. Save your preferences. If the only options are "Accept" and "Decline", choose "Decline" (if it implies declining optional cookies).
        - Refresh the cookie display in your developer tools. Take a screenshot and note any changes or new cookies.
    - **Second Interaction:** If you were able to customize, try to find the cookie settings again (often via a link in the footer like "Privacy Notice" or "Cookie Preferences").
        - This time, choose to "Accept all" or "Allow all cookies." Save your preferences.
        - Refresh the cookie display again. Take another screenshot and carefully note any new cookies that have appeared or any cookies that have changed compared to _both_ previous states (before consent and after rejecting/customizing).
5. For at least two _new_ cookies that appeared specifically after you "Accepted all" (or enabled more categories), record their names and try to infer their purpose (e.g., advertising, personalization, analytics) based on their name or the consent choices you made.
6. Answer the following:
    - What differences did you observe in the cookies:
        - Before interacting with the consent banner?
        - After rejecting/customizing optional cookies?
        - After accepting all cookies?
    - Which specific categories of cookies (e.g., advertising, personalization, analytics) do you think were added when you accepted all?
    - Why do websites like [Amazon.de](http://Amazon.de) implement these cookie consent mechanisms?

**Submission**

Submit a document (e.g., a text file or PDF) containing:

1. Your "before consent" notes and screenshot of the cookies.
2. Your "after rejecting/customizing optional cookies" notes and screenshot.
3. Your "after accepting all cookies" notes and screenshot, including your inferred purpose for at least two new/changed cookies from this step.
4. Your answers to the questions in step 6.
